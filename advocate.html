Know Your Rights

Section 498A (IPC) - Cruelty by Husband

Whoever, being the husband or the relative of the husband of a woman, subjects such woman to cruelty shall be punished with imprisonment for a term which may extend to three years and shall also be liable to fine. This is often used in dowry harassment cases.

Section 138 (NI Act) - Cheque Bounce

If a cheque issued for the discharge of debt is returned by the bank unpaid, it is a criminal offense punishable with imprisonment up to two years or with fine which may extend to twice the amount of the cheque, or with both.

Anticipatory Bail (Sec 438 CrPC)

Any person who has reason to believe that they may be arrested on accusation of having committed a non-bailable offense may apply to the High Court or the Court of Session for a direction that in the event of such arrest, they shall be released on bail.

Section 302 (IPC) - Murder

Whoever commits murder shall be punished with death or imprisonment for life, and shall also be liable to fine. This is the most serious offense in the Indian Penal Code.

Section 420 (IPC) - Cheating

Cheating and dishonestly inducing delivery of property. If someone deceives you to take money or property, this section applies. Punishment can extend up to 7 years.

Section 13B (HMA) - Mutual Divorce

Divorce by Mutual Consent. When both husband and wife agree to separate peacefully, this is the fastest way to get a divorce (usually takes 6 months).

NDPS Act - Drug Cases

The Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act. Strict laws against possession, sale, or consumption of drugs. Bail is difficult, requiring strong legal representation.

Section 376 (IPC) - Rape

Punishment for sexual assault. The law is very strict regarding crimes against women, with punishments ranging from 10 years to life imprisonment or death.

Section 323 (IPC) - Causing Hurt

Punishment for voluntarily causing hurt. Common in fights or physical altercations. It is a bailable offense but can be clubbed with serious charges.

Section 506 (IPC) - Criminal Intimidation

Punishment for criminal intimidation. If someone threatens to cause death, grievous hurt, or destruction of property, this section is applicable.

POCSO Act - Child Protection

Protection of Children from Sexual Offences. A special act to protect minors (under 18) from sexual assault and harassment. Cases are tried in special fast-track courts.
